Lasagna Recipe

The Classic Baked Lasagna

o achieve the rich, layered perfection shown in the image, the secret lies in the balance between a slow-cooked meat sauce and a velvety cheese topping. The lasagna is assembled with care, alternating between tender pasta sheets and a thick, savory Bolognese sauce. To get that signature golden-brown crust (often called the gratin), a generous layer of mozzarella and parmesan is added to the top, which bubbles and caramelizes under the high heat of the oven, creating those delicious charred spots seen in the dish.


The finishing touch, as captured in the photo, is the contrast of colors and textures. A sprig of fresh basil is placed on top just before serving, providing a burst of herbal freshness that cuts through the richness of the melted cheese and tomato sauce. Served in a rustic cast-iron or ceramic dish, this lasagna represents the ultimate "low and slow" cooking method, where the flavors meld together into a harmonious, cheesy masterpiece that is ready to be pulled apart with a fork.


Ingredients:

  • Lasagna Sheets: Oven-ready or par-boiled.
  • 500g Ground Beef: Cooked into a thick tomato-based sauce.
  • 2 cups Marinara or Bolognese Sauce: Rich and seasoned with herbs.
  • 300g Mozzarella Cheese: Shredded for that perfect melt.
  • 50g Parmesan Cheese: Grated for a salty, crispy crust.
  • Fresh Basil Leaves: For garnish and a pop of color.
  • Ricotta or Béchamel Sauce: (Optional) For the creamy white layers.

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Sauce: Simmer your ground beef with tomato sauce, onions, and garlic until thick and flavorful. A runny sauce will make the lasagna messy!
  2. Layer the Base: Spread a thin layer of sauce at the bottom of your baking dish. Place the first layer of pasta sheets on top.
  3. Build the Layers: Add a layer of meat sauce, followed by a layer of cheese (and béchamel if using). Repeat these layers until you reach the top of the dish.
  4. The Cheese Topping: For the final layer, cover the pasta with a heavy coating of mozzarella and parmesan. This is what creates that beautiful crust in the picture.
  5. Bake: Place in a preheated oven at 190°C (375°F) for about 30 to 40 minutes until the cheese is bubbling and has those distinct brown spots.
  6. Garnish: Let it sit for 10 minutes so the layers set, then top with fresh basil and serve.
